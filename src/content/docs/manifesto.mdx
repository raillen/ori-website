---
title: Language Manifesto
description: The core principles, motivation, and vision behind the Ori programming language.
---

**Ori Language** is born with a clear proposal: to place reading, comprehension, and predictability at the center of the programming experience.

In a landscape where many languages favor shortcuts, compact syntaxes, and implicit behaviors, Ori takes a different path. Its fundamental principle is simple: **code must be written to be understood**.

Ori is a **reading-first** language, created to value those who read code as much as those who write it. This means that clarity, explicit intent, and a predictable structure are not merely aesthetic choices, but foundations of the language itself.

Its objective is to reduce cognitive load, avoid unnecessary ambiguities, and make code more accessible, especially in projects that need to be maintained, reviewed, taught, and evolved over time.

Ori believes that good code should not depend on hidden tricks, excessive inference, or magical behavior. Every key decision must be visible in the source code itself. What happens must be understandable, traceable, and predictable.

Therefore, the language values:

* reading over short typing;
* clarity over cleverness;
* explicit behavior over magic;
* predictability over chaotic flexibility;
* composition over rigid inheritance;
* safety over dangerous convenience.

In Ori, concepts such as the absence of value and error handling must be expressed clearly. Instead of relying on `null`, `undefined`, or hidden exceptions, the language encourages explicit models, where the possibilities of failure or absence appear directly in the program's signature and flow.

Ori also advocates for a more accessible approach to different ways of thinking. Its syntax, diagnostics, and visual layout should favor reading, focus, and progressive comprehension, making programming less opaque and more human.

Ori does not seek to be a language of extreme shortcuts, nor a copy of existing languages. It does not intend to sacrifice readability for brevity, nor turn simplicity into limitation.

Its purpose is to be a clear, robust, compositional, and reliable language, capable of teaching, structuring, and sustaining real-world systems with less ambiguity and more intent.

**Ori exists to prove that a programming language can be technical without being obscure, powerful without being confusing, and expressive without ceasing to be readable.**

More than a tool for writing code, Ori is the defense of an idea:
**programming must be an act of clear communication between people, machines, and the future.**
